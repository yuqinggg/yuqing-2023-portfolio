Insight-to-Decision Trail

Australia is Singapore Airlines' second largest KrisFlyer membership base after Singapore, with over 1 million untapped KrisFlyer miles redeemed annually — yet Australian members had no dedicated KrisShop storefront to spend them on. Flight redemptions face limited inventory, creating a strategic opportunity to shift customer demand toward non-air redemptions through a localised KrisShop experience.

The design challenge was to translate this business opportunity into a coherent, insight-led product — one that surfaces miles as a meaningful currency for Australian shoppers across every touchpoint of the shopping journey.

Miles Redemption

Key Business Objective for the New Market

With over 1 million untapped KrisFlyer miles sitting in Australian accounts annually, miles redemption is the central value proposition of the Australian launch. Flight redemptions have limited inventory and high competition; KrisShop gives members a tangible, immediate way to spend their miles. The design ensures miles are not buried — they are surfaced as a primary currency throughout the entire user journey.

1. Miles Redemption Across the User Flow

Homepage — Miles balance is prominently featured on the homepage, immediately signposting to KrisFlyer members that their miles are spendable here. This anchors the value proposition from the moment a member lands on the site.

Campaign Page — A dynamic miles redemption landing page drives discovery, showcasing participating products and communicating the savings available when redeeming miles. This page adapts based on available promotions to keep the offer feeling current.

Product Listing Page (PLP) — Miles are presented as the primary currency for miles burners, with miles pricing displayed alongside AUD pricing on every product card. This makes it easy for members to compare and identify the best value for their miles.

Checkout — At checkout, members can apply their KrisFlyer miles for cost savings, with clear feedback on how many miles will be used and the resulting AUD saving. The flow is designed to reduce friction and encourage miles redemption at the point of purchase.

Localisation

2. Adapting for Australian Shoppers

Localising KrisShop for Australia went beyond currency conversion. Each checkout touchpoint was reviewed and adapted — from how prices display, to how miles and vouchers apply, to what members see after purchase.

AU LOCALISATION CHANGE

AU has a distinct redemption cap rule (10% of order value) absent from the SG flow. The constraint is surfaced inline — not hidden in T&Cs.

WHY IT MATTERS

Testing showed users felt deceived when the cap appeared only at the final step. Moving it here reduced drop-off and support tickets.

Seller Ecosystem

3. New Marketplace Capabilities

The Australian launch also introduced new seller ecosystem features to support a marketplace model, giving customers more transparency and control over their purchases.

  • Group Seller — Products from the same seller are grouped to streamline the browsing and checkout experience, reducing fragmentation when buying from multiple listings.
  • Contact Seller — Customers can reach sellers directly from the product page, supporting pre-purchase queries and building trust with marketplace listings.
  • Track Order — End-to-end order tracking is surfaced within the KrisShop platform, removing the need to navigate to external courier sites.
  • Return Request — A self-service return request flow is designed to meet Australian Consumer Law requirements, giving customers a clear and easy path for returns.
